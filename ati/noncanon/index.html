The Paracanonical Pali Texts巴利语三藏外文献

In the centuries immediately following the Buddha's death and the First Buddhist Council, arahants and scholar-monks began recording their own commentaries to the teachings of the Tipitaka. At first these commentaries, explanations, amplifications, meditation notes, and historical recollections were passed down orally within the monastic sangha but, like the Tipitaka itself, many began to be recorded in writing around the turn of the Common Era. Most of these early texts — primarily written in Sinhala — remained for centuries tucked away in forest monasteries and temples in Sri Lanka, accessible only to a few Sinhala scholars. It wasn't until these scattered fragments were translated into Pali and collated into coherent texts (most notably by the great Indian scholar Buddhaghosa (5th c)) that they became available to the wider Theravadan world. 在佛陀入灭及第一次结集后的数百年间，阿罗汉与博学比丘们开始为三藏教法撰写各自的注释。起初，这些注释、解说、增补、禅修笔记与历史追述皆在僧团内部口耳相传，但如同三藏本身一样，其中许多内容也在公元纪年前后开始被书写记录下来。这些早期文献大多以僧伽罗文写成，数百年间深藏于斯里兰卡的林中寺院，仅有少数僧伽罗学者得以接触。直到这些散佚的片段被译为巴利语并汇编成书（其中最著名的整理者是五世纪印度大学者觉音尊者），它们才得以传播至更广大的上座部世界。

Since then, these texts — variously labeled "non-canonical," "extra-canonical, or "post-canonical" — have come to be regarded as essential supplements to the teachings of the Pali canon itself. So treasured, for example, is the Milindapañha that it has even been subsumed in the Burmese edition of the Tipitaka; and in some parts of the Theravadan world Buddhaghosa's monumental Visuddhimagga is regarded as a more definitive guide to Buddhist meditation practice than even the Tipitaka itself. Taken together, the Tipitaka and much of this non-canonical Pali literature (in particular the Commentaries) constitute the complete body of classical Theravada texts. 此后，这些文献——或称"非三藏""藏外"或"后三藏"文献——逐渐被视为巴利三藏教法不可或缺的补充。例如《弥兰陀王问经》备受推崇，以至于被缅甸版三藏收录其中；而在上座部世界的某些地区，觉音尊者的巨著《清净道论》甚至被视为比三藏本身更权威的禅修指南。三藏与其中大部分非三藏巴利文献（尤其是注疏）合在一起，共同构成了上座部古典文献的完整体系。

For a more detailed overview, see "Beyond the Tipitaka: A Field Guide to Post-canonical Pali Literature." 更详细的概述请参见《超越三藏：巴利语后典籍文献指南》。

    Visuddhimagga — The Path of Purification: The Classic Manual of Buddhist Doctrine and Meditation, translated from the Pali by Bhikkhu Ñāṇamoli《清净道论》（Visuddhimagga）——佛教教理与禅修的经典手册，向智比丘（Bhikkhu Ñāṇamoli）英译
    "The Visuddhimagga is the 'great treatise' of Theravada Buddhism, an encyclopedic manual of Buddhist doctrine and meditation written in the fifth century by the great Buddhist commentator, Bhadantacariya Buddhaghosa. The author's intention in composing this book is to organize the various teachings of the Buddha, found throughout the Pali Canon, into a clear and comprehensive path leading to the final Buddhist goal, Nibbana, the state of complete purification." "《清净道论》是上座部佛教的'大论'，由五世纪伟大的佛教注释家觉音尊者撰写，是一部百科全书式的佛教教理与禅修手册。作者著述此书的用意，是将巴利三藏各处所载的佛陀教法，整理为一条清晰完整、通向佛教最终目标——涅槃（彻底清净之境）——的修行道路。"
