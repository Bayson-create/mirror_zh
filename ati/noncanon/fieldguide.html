Beyond the Tipitaka: A Field Guide to Post-canonical Pali Literature超越三藏：巴利语后典籍文献指南

A quick glance through the pages of the Pali Text Society's publications catalog should be enough to convince anyone that there is much more to classical Pali literature than the Tipitaka alone. Intermingled with the familiar Nikayas, Vinaya texts, and Abhidhamma are scores of titles with long, scarcely-pronounceable Pali names. Although many western students of Buddhism may be unacquainted with these works, these books have for centuries played a crucial role in the development of Buddhist thought and practice across Asia and, ultimately, the West. In fact, in some countries they are as deeply treasured as the suttas themselves. But what are these ancient books, and what relevance do they have to the western student of Buddhism in the 21st century? I hope that this short document will provide enough of a road map to help orient the interested student as he or she sets out to explore this vast corpus of important Buddhist literature. 只需快速翻阅巴利圣典学会（Pali Text Society）的出版目录，便足以让任何人相信：古典巴利文献远不止三藏一途。在人们熟悉的诸尼柯耶、律典与论藏之间，还夹杂着数十部名称冗长、几乎难以读出的巴利典籍。虽然许多西方佛学学人对这些著作陌生，但这些典籍数百年来一直在亚洲乃至西方佛教思想与修行的发展中扮演着关键角色，在某些国家甚至与经藏本身同受珍视。那么，这些古老典籍究竟是什么？对21世纪的西方佛学学人又有何意义？希望这篇短文能提供足够的路线图，帮助有兴趣的读者踏上探索这一庞大而重要的佛教文献宝库的旅程。

This article is in two parts. The Introduction provides historical background to the texts and offers some thoughts on why these texts are so valuable to the Theravada tradition. The Field Guide is essentially an annotated table of contents, in which I borrow heavily from a variety of sources to describe each text. 本文分为两部分。"导论"部分交代这些典籍的历史背景，并说明它们对上座部传统的价值所在；"文献指南"部分本质上是一份附有说明的书目，其中对各典籍的介绍大量借鉴了多种参考资料。

Introduction导论

The origins of the post-canonical texts后三藏文献的起源

The Tipitaka (Pali canon) assumed its final form at the Third Buddhist Council (ca. 250 BCE) and was first committed to writing sometime in the 1st c. BCE. Shortly thereafter Buddhist scholar-monks in Sri Lanka and southern India began to amass a body of secondary literature: commentaries on the Tipitaka itself, historical chronicles, textbooks, Pali grammars, articles by learned scholars of the past, and so on. Most of these texts were written in Sinhala, but because Pali — not Sinhala — was the lingua franca of Theravada, few Buddhist scholars outside Sri Lanka could study them. It wasn't until the 5th c. CE, when the Indian monk Buddhaghosa began the laborious task of collating the ancient Sinhala commentaries and translating them into Pali, that these books first became accessible to non-Sinhala speakers around the Buddhist world. These commentaries (Atthakatha) offer meticulously detailed explanations and analyses — phrase-by-phrase and word-by-word — of the corresponding passages in the Tipitaka. 三藏（巴利圣典）在第三次结集（约公元前250年）时定型，并于公元前一世纪首次被书写记录下来。此后不久，斯里兰卡与南印度的博学比丘们开始积累起大量二手文献：三藏本身的注释、历史编年史、教科书、巴利语语法书，以及历代学者的论著等。这些文献大多以僧伽罗文写成，但由于上座部的通用语是巴利语而非僧伽罗语，斯里兰卡以外的佛教学者鲜少能够研读。直到公元五世纪，印度比丘觉音尊者着手将古代僧伽罗注释汇集并译为巴利语，这些典籍才首度为整个佛教世界中不谙僧伽罗语的学人所知晓。这些注释（义注，Atthakatha）针对三藏相应段落逐句逐词地提供了极为细致的解说与分析。

After Buddhaghosa the catalog of post-canonical Pali literature continued to grow with the addition of commentaries by both Buddhadatta (5th c.) and Dhammapala (6th c.), and sub-commentaries (Tika) by Dhammapala on several of Buddhaghosa's Atthakathas. During this time, and in the centuries that followed, other writers prepared Pali translations of additional early Sinhala texts. These ranged from poetic hymns in celebration of the Buddha, to chronicles tracing the first millennium of Buddhist history, to detailed Abhidhamma textbooks. Most of the major post-canonical works, including the sub-commentaries, were completed by the 12th c. 觉音尊者之后，后三藏巴利文献的目录持续扩充，佛授（Buddhadatta，五世纪）与法护（Dhammapala，六世纪）相继添加了新的注释，法护更为觉音的几部义注撰写了复注（Tika）。在此期间及此后数百年间，还有其他作者将更多早期僧伽罗文献译为巴利语，内容涵盖歌颂佛陀的诗篇、追溯佛教前千年历史的编年史，乃至详尽的阿毗达摩教科书。大多数重要的后三藏著作（包括复注）都在十二世纪之前完成。

Why these texts matter这些文献为何重要

Post-canonical Pali literature supplements the Tipitaka in several important ways. First, the chronicles and commentaries provide a vital thread of temporal continuity that links us, via the persons and historical events of the intervening centuries, to the Tipitaka's world of ancient India. A Tipitaka without this accompanying historical thread would forever be an isolated anachronism to us, its message lost in clouds of myth and fable, its pages left to gather dust in museum display cases alongside ancient Egyptian mummies. These texts remind us that the Dhamma is not an artifact but a practice, and that we belong to a long line of seekers who have endeavored, through patient practice, to keep these teachings alive. 后三藏巴利文献在几个重要方面补充了三藏。首先，编年史与注释提供了一条至关重要的时间脉络，透过历代人物与历史事件，将我们与三藏所处的古印度世界联系起来。若没有这条伴随的历史线索，三藏对我们而言将永远是一件孤立的、不合时宜的古物，其讯息湮没在神话传说的迷雾中，其篇页如同古埃及木乃伊一般，只能在博物馆展柜中蒙尘。这些文献提醒我们：法不是文物，而是一种修行；我们属于一长串求道者的行列，他们历经耐心的修持，努力使这些教法生生不息。

Second, almost everything we know today about the early years of Buddhism comes to us from these post-canonical books. Though the archaeological evidence from that era is scant and the Tipitaka itself contains only a handful of passages describing events that followed the Buddha's death, the commentaries and chronicles contain a wealth of historical information with which we are able to partially reconstruct the early history of Buddhism. The texts illuminate a host of important historical events and trends: how the Tipitaka came to be preserved orally; when it was first written down, and why; how the Tipitaka came close to extinction; how the Buddha's teachings spread across south Asia; how and when the various schools and factions within Buddhism arose; and so on. Any practitioner, of any century, stands to benefit from understanding how the early Buddhists lived, how they put the Buddha's teachings into practice, what challenges they faced. Without the post-canonical texts important lessons like these — if not the Tipitaka itself — might have been lost forever to the mists of time. 其次，我们如今对佛教早期历史的了解，几乎全部来自这些后三藏典籍。尽管那个时代的考古证据稀少，三藏本身也只有寥寥几段描述佛陀入灭后事件的文字，但注释与编年史保存了大量历史资讯，使我们得以部分重建佛教的早期历史：三藏如何以口传方式保存下来、何时以及为何首次被书写记录、三藏又如何险些失传、佛陀的教法如何传播至南亚各地、佛教内部各部派如何以及何时兴起等等。任何时代的修行者，若能了解早期佛教徒的生活方式、他们如何将佛陀教法付诸实践、面临过怎样的挑战，都将从中受益。若没有这些后三藏文献，这些重要的教训——乃至三藏本身——都可能早已湮没于历史的迷雾之中。

Third, these texts — particularly the commentaries — help us make sense of the suttas and give us clues about their context that we might otherwise miss. For example, the famous Satipatthana Sutta (MN 10) is popularly cited today as evidence that all one needs to achieve Awakening is a week or two of unrelenting mindfulness practice. But the commentary suggests another viewpoint: the Buddha's audience for this particular discourse were already well established in their practice of mindfulness and virtue — a point not apparent from the text of the sutta itself. The commentary thus reminds us that there are some important fundamentals to be developed before one undertakes intensive meditation practice. 第三，这些文献——尤其是注释——有助于我们理解经文，并提供经文本身可能遗漏的背景线索。例如，著名的《念处经》（中部第10经）如今常被引用，作为"只需一两周不间断的正念修习即可证得觉悟"的依据。但注释书提供了另一种视角：它指出，佛陀在这部经中的听众其实早已在正念与戒行上有深厚的修习基础，并非从零开始接触禅修——这一点从经文本身是看不出来的。因此，注释提醒我们，在进行密集禅修之前，仍有一些重要的基础功夫需要先行培育。

Finally, the commentaries often contain magnificent stories to illustrate and amplify upon points of Dhamma that are made in the suttas. Commentarial stories like these offer valuable Dhamma teachings in their own right. 最后，注释书中常收录许多精彩的故事，用以阐释与深化经文中所讲的法义要点。这类注释故事本身即具有宝贵的教导价值。

The authority of the texts文献的权威性

One might reasonably wonder: how can a collection of texts written a thousand years after the Buddha's death possibly represent his teachings reliably? First of all, although many of these texts were indeed first written in Pali a thousand years after the Buddha, most Sinhala versions upon which they were based were written much earlier, having themselves been passed down via an ancient and reliable oral tradition. But by the argument that early texts based on hearsay must be suspect, we should reject the entire oral tradition — and hence the entire Tipitaka itself, which similarly emerged from an oral tradition long after the Buddha's death. Surely that is taking things too far. 人们或许会合理地质疑：一部在佛陀入灭千年之后才写成的文献集，怎么可能可靠地代表佛陀的教法？首先，虽然这些文献确实大多是在佛灭千年后才首次以巴利语写定，但其所依据的僧伽罗文本大多成书早得多，且本身也是经由古老而可靠的口传传统流传下来的。但若以"早期文本仅凭口耳相传故而可疑"为由质疑它们，那么我们就该否定整个口传传统——从而也否定三藏本身，因为三藏同样是在佛灭许久之后才从口传传统中产生的成文典籍。这样的质疑未免太过。

But what of the credentials of the commentators themselves: can their words be trusted? In addition to living a monastic life immersed in Dhamma, the compilers of the commentaries possessed unimpeachable literary credentials: intimate acquaintance with the Tipitaka, mastery of the Pali and Sinhala languages, and expert skill in the art of careful scholarship. We have no reason to doubt either their abilities or the sincerity of their intentions. 那么注释作者本身的资历又如何呢？他们的说法是否可信？这些注释的编纂者除了终身浸润于法义的僧团生活外，还具备无可挑剔的学术资历：精熟三藏、通晓巴利语与僧伽罗语、并具备审慎治学的专业技艺。我们没有理由怀疑他们的能力，也没有理由怀疑他们意图的真诚。

And what of their first-hand understanding of Dhamma: if the commentators were scholars first and foremost, would they have had sufficient meditative experience to write with authority on the subject of meditation? This is more problematic. It is noteworthy that the most significant discrepancies between the Canon and its commentaries concern meditation — in particular, the relationship between concentration meditation and insight. The question of the authority of the post-canonical texts thus remains a point of controversy within Theravada Buddhism. 至于他们对法义的亲身体证又如何？如果这些注释者首先是学者，他们是否具备足够的禅修经验，得以就禅修议题写出权威之言？这一点更值得商榷。值得注意的是，三藏与其注释之间最显著的分歧恰恰出现在禅修议题上——尤其是止禅与观禅之间的关系。因此，后三藏文献的权威性问题至今仍是上座部佛教内部争议不休的话题。

It is important to remember that the ultimate function of the post-canonical texts is — like that of the Tipitaka itself — to assist the student in the quest for nibbana, the highest goal of Buddhist practice. Concerns about authorship and authority recede when the texts are subjected to the same healthy skeptical attitude and empirical approach that should be familiar to every student of the suttas. If a commentary sheds light on a murky corner of a sutta or helps us understand a subtle point of Vinaya or of Abhidhamma, or if the chronicles remind us that we hold the future history of Dhamma in our hands, then to that extent they help us clear the path ahead. 重要的是要记住：后三藏文献的终极功能——如同三藏本身一样——是协助修行者追求佛教修行的最高目标：涅槃。只要以每位经典学人都应熟悉的健康怀疑态度与实证方法来对待这些文献，关于作者身份与权威性的疑虑便会退居次要。若一部注释能够澄清经文中某个含糊之处，或帮助我们理解律藏或论藏中的微妙论点，抑或编年史提醒我们：法义的未来历史掌握在我们自己手中——那么在此意义上，它们便已助我们廓清了前行的道路。
