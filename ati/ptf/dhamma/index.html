The Dhamma法

A Gradual Training次第教法

The Dhamma, the truth taught by the Buddha, is uncovered gradually through sustained practice. The Buddha made clear many times that Awakening does not occur like a bolt out of the blue to the untrained and unprepared mind. Rather, it culminates a long journey of many stages. 法，即佛陀所教导的真理，须透过持续的修习而逐步揭示。佛陀曾多次明示：觉悟并不会如晴天霹雳般，突然降临于未经训练、毫无准备的心；相反，它是历经多个阶段的漫长旅程所臻于圆满的成果。

经文以海洋为喻（Ud 5.5）：大海由浅入深、渐次倾斜，唯有经过长远距离才骤然陡降；同样，此法与律也是循序渐进地训练、修习、进展，唯有经过长久修习才能豁然贯通、证入智慧。另一段经文（MN 70）说明：智慧的证得并非一蹴而就，而是经由渐次的训练、行动与修习而来——由生起信心，到亲近善知识，到倾听教法，到忆持教法，到深入其义，到深思而认同，到生起意欲，到审度思惟，到发起精进，最终以身证得究竟真理，并以智慧洞见之。

The Buddha's teachings are infused with this notion of gradual development. His method of "gradual instruction" (anupubbi-katha), which appears in various forms in countless suttas, always follows the same arc: he guides newcomers from first principles through progressively more advanced teachings, all the way to the fulfillment of the Four Noble Truths and the full realization of nibbana. 佛陀的教法始终贯穿着这种循序渐进的理念。他的"次第说法"（anupubbi-katha）以各种形式出现于无数经文中，其脉络始终如一：引导初学者由基本原理入手，逐步深入更高阶的教法，直至圆满四圣谛、彻底证悟涅槃。

经文（Ud 5.3）记载了一个典型例证：佛陀以其心遍观法会大众之心，见麻风病患苏跋陀坐于会中，判断此人能够理解法义，便针对他依次讲说布施、持戒、生天之论，阐明欲望的过患、堕落与染污，以及出离的利益。待见其心已柔软、无障碍、欢喜、明净，方讲说唯有觉悟者才宣说的法——苦、集、灭、道。正如洁净无染的布料才能妥善染色，苏跋陀就座之时，远尘离垢的法眼便在他心中生起："凡有生起之法，皆会灭去。"

At each stage of this "gradual training" (anupubbi-sikkha), the practitioner discovers a new and important dimension of the law of cause-and-effect — kamma, the cornerstone of Right View. It is thus a very useful organizing framework with which to view the entirety of the Buddha's teachings. 在"次第修学"（anupubbi-sikkha）的每个阶段，修行者都会发现因果法则——业（kamma，正见的基石）——的一个新的重要面向。因此，这一框架是理解佛陀全部教法的一条极为实用的组织线索。

The gradual training begins with the practice of generosity, which helps begin the long process of weakening the unawakened practitioner's habitual tendencies to cling — to views, to sensuality, and to unskillful modes of thought and behavior. This is followed by the development of virtue, the basic level of sense-restraint that helps the practitioner develop a healthy and trustworthy sense of self. The peace of mind born from this level of self-respect provides the foundation for all further progress along the path. The practitioner now understands that some kinds of happiness are deeper and more dependable than anything that sense-gratification can ever provide; the happiness born of generosity and virtue can even lead to rebirth in heaven — either literal or metaphorical. But eventually the practitioner begins to recognize the intrinsic drawbacks of even this kind of happiness: as good as rebirth in wholesome states may be, the happiness it brings is not a true and lasting one, for it relies on conditions over which he or she ultimately has no control. This marks a crucial turning point in the training, when the practitioner begins to grasp that true happiness will never be found in the realm of the physical and sensual world. The only possible route to an unconditioned happiness lies in renunciation, in turning away from the sensual realm, by trading the familiar, lower forms of happiness for something far more rewarding and noble. Now, at last, the practitioner is ripe to receive the teachings on the Four Noble Truths, which spell out the course of mental training required to realize the highest happiness: nibbana. 次第训练始于布施的修习，这有助于开启一个漫长的过程：逐步削弱未觉悟者对见解、感官欲乐及不善思想行为模式的惯性执着。其后是持戒的培育，即感官节制的基础层次，帮助修行者建立起健康而可靠的自我感。由此自重而生的内心平静，是继续在道上前行的一切基础。修行者此时明白，有些快乐比感官满足所能提供的一切都更深刻、更可靠；由布施与持戒而生的快乐，甚至能带来（无论是字面意义还是譬喻意义上的）生天之果。但修行者终将认识到，即便是这种快乐也存在内在的过患：即便投生于善趣是好事，这种快乐终究并非真实恒常，因为它依赖于自己终究无法掌控的条件。这标志着训练中的一个关键转折点——修行者开始领悟到，真正的快乐永远无法在物质与感官的世界中寻得。通往无为之乐的唯一道路，在于出离，即转离感官领域，以熟悉而较低层次的快乐，换取远为珍贵而高尚的事物。至此，修行者终于成熟到足以领受四圣谛的教法——这正是证得最高快乐（涅槃）所需的心智训练历程。

Many Westerners first encounter the Buddha's teachings on meditation retreats, which typically begin with instructions in how to develop the skillful qualities of right mindfulness and right concentration. It is worth noting that, as important as these qualities are, the Buddha placed them towards the very end of his gradual course of training. The meaning is clear: to reap the most benefit from meditation practice, to bring to full maturity all the qualities needed for Awakening, the fundamental groundwork must not be overlooked. There is no short-cutting this process. 许多西方人最初是透过禅修营接触佛陀的教法，而这类课程通常一开始就教导如何培育正念与正定这两项善巧品质。值得注意的是，尽管这些品质极为重要，佛陀却将它们安排在其次第训练历程的末端。这一安排的含义很明确：若要从禅修中获得最大裨益、令觉悟所需的一切品质臻于圆满，就不能忽略最基础的功夫。这个过程没有捷径可走。
